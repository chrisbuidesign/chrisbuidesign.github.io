Alert Banner

Role

Visual + Interaction Designer

Timeline

6 months

Format

Web

Context

In order to transition from an outdated design system to a fresher one, I contributed to the creation of a fully custom design system. I was involved with the design and specification of components such as alert banners, toast messages, and empty and error states. Alert banners, especially, were a high priority item due to their frequency of use.

In addition to owning the design of these components, I was also responsible for defining their interactions in the early stages of the design system. I defined the rules for when to use them and how they could be used in a mockup.

Goals & Challenges

Design an alert banner that supports a variety of messages and redirect situations.

Drawing Inspiration

During the research phase of the project, I looked into the design systems of other successful brands such as the Salesforce Lightning, IBM Carbon, and GitHub Primer Design systems. I noted common patterns among them and studied the differences in how they each communicated information. Eventually I had a collection of designs I could reference from and created a set of options for review.

The second to last option won the vote. Simple is best.

Defining a Component

Once the initial design was determined, some tweaks were made to integrate it into the design system, such as adding a bit of a radius to the corners and updating its color to align with the new color palette. Each part of the component was broken up and specified in the visual spec for dev handoff.
